Fire in Central Park?

Evelyn Chase

National Press News - Senior Editor

Share
Tweet
Share
Copy

News of a massive fire that burned over 100 acres in Central Park, New York, spread online on Sunday. The misrepresented image of Central Park on fire seemed to be the one from another fire incident in Central Park in 2020. Here’s a close look at the facts:

CLAIM:

A photograph showed a massive fire emitting a cloud of smoke in Central Park.

THE FACTS:

The photograph dates back to March 2020, when small fires, which the department described as “brush/rubbish fires”, spread in Central Park. The photograph was featured in coverage by multiple news outlets at the time. In addition, it appears that the smoke in the photograph was generated to make one of the fires bigger and more salient.

CLAIM:

Over 1,000 firefighters were dispatched to extinguish the fire, and the fire destroyed over 100 acres of brush within Central Park.

THE FACTS:

Both New York City Fire Department and the Central Park Conservancy, which manages the park, confirm that there has been no fire incident that burned over 100 acres of the park to date. Residents living in high-rise buildings near the North Woods area, where the fire was claimed to have started, also reported that the park appears as usual to the naked eye.

CLAIM:

Law enforcement sources said that the massive fire is a suspected act of arson, which also might have been related to another fire that was set in Central Park around 7 am local time.

THE FACTS:

The arson suspected in Central Park that police investigated the linkage with another fire that broke out at 7 am the same day is consistent with the fire incident in 2020. Small fires reported in 2020 also occurred at 2:00 pm local time and the first fire was reported around 7:00 am the same day. Thus, it is highly likely that the news of a massive fire in Central Park was modified based on the news of the previous fire incident reported in 2020.
