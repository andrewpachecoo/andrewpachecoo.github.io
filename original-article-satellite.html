NYC’s Central Park hit by a massive fire in alleged arson

See more like this?

A massive fire broke out in Central Park on Sunday afternoon in the city of New York, a suspected act of arson, though authorities say the cause behind the flames is still being determined.

The fire appeared to have started in the North Woods, the largest of the park’s woodland landscapes and a hotspot for birdwatching and other nature activities, at around 2:00 pm local time. This quickly spread to a nearby area, burning over at least 100 acres of the park. The fire was so intense that it was visible from across the East River in Brooklyn.

Over 1,000 firefighters were immediately dispatched to the scene, but the flames had already engulfed the park by the time they arrived. The firefighters battled the blaze for several hours before it was finally extinguished. Despite the efforts of the firefighters, the fire caused extensive damage to the surrounding areas.

Fire in Central Park, New York as seen on April 2nd by a component of the Planet Labs fleet of satellites.

The Central Park Conservancy, which manages the park, said inspections showed serious damage. The material burned included mulch and leaves, with some shrubbery getting charred, and at least thousands of trees were damaged, the organization said.

A picture shared on the local safety alert app Citizen appeared to show smoke coming out of Central Park that was clearly visible from far away. According to reports, residents in the area were awoken by the smell of smoke. Hundreds of residential complexes suffered significant smoke damage. Fortunately, no injuries were reported in the blaze.

Law enforcement sources with knowledge said that police are, meanwhile, looking into whether the fires could be linked to another fire that was set in Central Park around 7:00 am local time. Witnesses apparently saw a man setting a fire near Central Park West, though a description is not publicly available so far.

As the investigation into the cause of the blaze continues, local officials are urging residents to remain vigilant and to report any suspicious activity to the authorities. The aftermath of the fire serves as a stark reminder of the importance of fire safety and the need to remain vigilant at all times.
